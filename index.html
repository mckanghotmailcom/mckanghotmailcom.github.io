Get to know me!

I have 18 years of research and teaching experience as an applied mathematician, applied statistician, and mathematical biologist. My research is focused on mathematical biology, which is grounded in theories of partial differential equations, dynamical systems, probability, statistics, and scientific computation. I am particularly interested in systems biology, with an emphasis on estimating binding diffusion kinetic rates and spatiotemporal modeling of biological systems. My work has been published extensively in peer-reviewed papers, with over 1,000 citations in the field. Additionally, I have a patent for a statistical tool that measures anomalous diffusion using fluorescence microscopy.
